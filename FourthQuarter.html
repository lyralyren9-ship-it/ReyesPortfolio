Event: BOTB

BATTLE OF THE BANDS!! (CHAMPIOOONNNNSSSSSS!!!!)

THIS WAS THE BEST BAND EVENT, PERIOD. EVERY SINGLE BAND DID SO WELL, BUT OMMMMGGGGGG PADAYOOONNNNN!!!!! I've been an avid Padayon fan since they first formed, and that's all because Sam is literally my most precious friend. She was literally my first friend EVER in Grade 7, and I WAS HERS TOO. I can't say I've always had her back since she's had her controversies here and there, but overall, she's grown into a so much better person from them and it makes me so proud knowing that while I wasn't always by her side, I was always there for her when I could. She's an amazing drummer, and an even more amazing friend. The other band members are amazing as well, even if I may not be AS close to them, I still consider most, if not, all of them as my friends. They're all amazing people, and truly deserve this win. Can't wait for the next Batte of The Bands to record them myself this time!!
What'd I learn in this event? Padayon is the best band in LPSci, and all the ates and kuyas in Grade 10 can beat me up for it. Sue me. For real though, What I did learn in this event is how much music really matters to these people, and to not judge a judge, because they're judges for a reason I learned that all the way in Street Dance as well. This event was full of so much passsion and love from each band, and it was so sick seeing bands from last year go again. How I'd apply this in real life is by having the same passion for what I love doing, that being drawing! Ofcourse, it takes discipline and such to love and learn tolove your hobbies while actually doing them instead of the idea of them, but still! How I'd teach this to a friend is that you just gotta watch BOTB for yourself with a fresh and open mind man. REALLY see the people and the performers and how it might look from their perspective. This could be one of the most climactic moments in their current time in LPsci.
The importance of this event is that it's one of the sickest and most climactic events ending the school year off with a bang without cramming it. Mostly because it's on a Saturday, BUT STILL. This event means so much to me as a whole because I know people who've never gone to a singular BOTB in their LPSci lives ever, and this being their first one. Ofcourse, I already had prior motivation since Sam's been in all of them, BUT STILL. This event is amazing, uniting people through music and support for their bands. It's truly an amazing event, and I can't wait for next year.

Credits to the Parents and Jade Imperial for the vid and pics!

Event: Folk Dance

Folk Dance

This event is something I don't really think I can speak much about since I don't have that much or as much attatchment to this event compared to BOTB, so I'll just get to the guide questions. What I think I learned from that event is how diverse the folk dances we have as Filipinos truly are. I really found the dances performed really entertaining and interesting with how different each of them were. How I'd apply this in my life is just by appreciatinhg the culture we have as Filipino's and our culture, especially considering our history. Appreciating our culture is extremely imprtant with how much it's dying in our day and age, succumbing to our colonial mindset, so it's important thatwe remember and carry our culture with us throughout our lives and appreciate it. How I'd teach this to a friend is to just appreciate our culture, and to appreciate their own culture because that's one of the things I believe makes us ourselves. This event overall was important because it highlighted how important our roots in our culture really is to us as people.

Credits to Jade Imperial for Pics!!

Event: Street Dance

Street Dance

I didn't really care for this event either outside of just supporting my friends and section, so I'll make this one quick aswell. What I learned from this event, I don't even know myself other than it just being really cool. Other than that though, it shows the other parts of modern Filipino culture and what we have right now as an evolving society in 2026. It shows how much we've changed and what influence we took from popular media or whatever else we thought looked cool, and made it our own. How I'd apply this in real life is that depsite it not really being my style, I still appreciate it for what it is and in general, how it looks since it's still a style that's widely popular in the Phillipines. I like it. How I'd teach this to a friends is that you just gotta be more open-minded in general to appreciate this kind of stuff. If seeing it makes you mad or anything else negative, just ignore it. It really isn't that hard. Why it's important is for the same reason the Folk Dance was important, being that it represents modern Filipino culture in how we've evolved and in what ways. It practices the culture in not just this, but in Folk Dance too. Overall for me, Both of those events mean and represent our culture and who we are as Filipinos.
